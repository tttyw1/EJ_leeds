The software visualizes the distribution of air pollution environments in Leeds in 2021, as well as environmental inequalities.

The map Layer uses the 2021 Leeds Lower Layer Super Output Area (LSOA) level vector layer, with a total of 488 regions

The air pollution data in this study is sourced from the Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs (Defra) of the UK. Six common air pollutants were selected: PM10, PM2.5, SO2, NOx, NO2 and Benzene.

For demographic data and socioeconomic data, the Index of Multiple Deprivation (IMD) and income deprivation, education deprivation, health deprivation and disability were selected in this study. The data used is from the 2011 LSOAs, with a total of 482 areas in Leeds. After integrating the 2011 data with the 2021 data, 15 areas were excluded from the map.

The study selects the percentage of children and elderly people and the population from different racial groups. These data comes from 2021 census data.
